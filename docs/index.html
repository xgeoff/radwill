04 // Historical Record

A banking house shaped by railways, industry, and patient capital.

Commonwealth traces its origins to New York in 1881, when early financing for rail corridors, warehouses, and industrial expansion established a habit of careful underwriting and long memory.

Founding Lineage

Rudolf Wladyslaw Radwill, descended from a Polish-Lithuanian noble line, founded the bank with an emphasis on merchant discipline, reserve prudence, and inherited duty.

1881

Founded in New York

The Commonwealth banking house is established to connect merchant capital, rail expansion, and Atlantic trade.

1894

Rail corridor finance expands

Commonwealth develops a reputation in rail refinancing, equipment trusts, depot redevelopment, and freight corridor improvements across the Northeast.

1918

War finance and treasury placements

The firm deepens relationships with industrial and civic institutions through bond distribution and reserve placements.

1947

Postwar industrial modernization

Credit and advisory services support port logistics, machine works, utilities, and manufacturers entering the postwar expansion.

1979

International platform formalized

Private capital and institutional advisory mandates expand through London and Geneva representation.

2026

Modern Commonwealth, enduring stewardship

The public bank continues under the long-range ownership of R. W. Radwill & Co., modernizing with restraint.
